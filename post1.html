An Introduction Dreaming in Black & White: American Film Noir

Written by Catherine

“The streets were dark with something more than night.” – Raymond Chandler

You see, if you haven’t heard—I have this cool multimedia film archive that showcases American film noir. It's an exciting journey that takes you from pre-war Hollywood to the gritty world of post-war crime, exploring all the techniques used in this fascinating genre. Film preservation is something I'm really passionate about, even though it's often undervalued. That's why I created this archive—to help people, especially the younger generation, understand the importance of preserving our film history.

In this exhibit, I've put together a collection of physical and digital artifacts that represent the iconic elements of film noir. You'll see everything from the allure of death to the dreamlike states that often feature in these films, not to mention the unforgettable femme fatales. There are archive materials from the Academy Archival Foundation, USC Warner Bros., and the UCLA Special Collections Library. It's a feast for the eyes, trust me!

But that's not all. As part of the exhibit, I've also arranged a screening of my 16mm short film called "La Paloma." It's a fantastic imitation of the noir style, capturing the same cinematography and narrative archetypes that define the genre. It’s inspired by classics like Double Indemnity, The Maltese Falcon, The Big Heat, and many more. It's a must-see for any film noir enthusiast.

Now, let's rewind a bit. May 12th, marked the first anniversary of my honors thesis. Can you believe it? It's been a year already… Funnily enough, my thesis didn't start off centered around noir. Originally, I was all about film preservation and had this grand idea of opening a gallery. But as summer rolled around, I realized that film noir was what really sparked my interest. I knew it wasn’t going to be easy though, and a year later I can officially say that it was definitely not easy.

However, choosing the genre wasn't too difficult for me. I've been a fan of German expressionist and surrealist works for a long time, so noir was a natural fit. At first, I toyed with the idea of focusing on horror for my thesis (maybe that's something for the future), but ultimately, noir won me over. It has such a fascinating history behind it, and I couldn't wait to delve deeper and learn more. I hope that you will find this fascinating too!

So, there you have it! My journey from film preservation enthusiast to curator of an exciting film noir archive. It's been quite a ride, and I'm thrilled to share it with all of you. If you're in the area, make sure to swing by and experience the magic of film noir firsthand.

You won't be disappointed!
